April 18, 2011 : Surviving the Streets of Long Beach is not an easy task as was evident this past weekend as GMG was racing in both the ALMS GTC Class as well as the Pirelli World Challenge. 5 cars in WC made for a busy weekend with the single ALMS entry running on Sat. The GMG/Spectra Resources Porsche didn't even have a chance to repeat their podium effort from 2010 as they were taken out on the first lap in turn one. Needless to say the team was extremely disappointed but now the focus was on the WC race on Sunday. James Sofronas led the charge for GMG with a 2nd place finish in the GT Class after falling to 9th on the 1st lap and charging through the field, with Tomy Drissi, Dino Crescentini and Brent Holden finishing 9th, 12th and 16th respectively. Dino had another strong run at Long Beach and was up to 3rd with 5 laps to go when a GTS Mustang turned in front of him and took Dino out. Drissi, after getting a stop and go for rolling on the start, picked his way through the traffic to finish a respectable 9th place while Brent Holden was headed for a top ten until contact with the turn 11 wall sidelined him for the race.

Unfortunately, Bill Ziegler in the GMG/Swisher Mitsubishi Evo was not able to race on Sunday due to an on track incident during practice on Sat.

GMG's 2nd place finish at the Long Beach Grand Prix showed further proof that their World Challenge line of performance products, which are developed for and tested in this series, set the industry standard. Having survived a brutal race and being the top finishing Porsche in the field, once again demonstrates GMG's dominance when it comes designing and building a reliable package for street or track use.GMG looks forward to it's next event at the wide open Miller Motorsports Park in Tooele, UTAH in less than 2 weeks.
