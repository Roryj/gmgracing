James Sofronas, driver of the #14 GMG 997 GT3 Cup car, finished 2nd at Miller Motorsports Park and in doing so captured the Championship Points lead in the Pirelli World Challenge Series.

Starting on the pole based on Drivers' Points after qualifying was snowed out Friday afternoon, James took off on the first lap and led the way for the next 12. Lapped traffic proved to be the culprit as James came up on a slower GTS car in turn 3 causing him to get out of the power, allowing the Viper to use its torque to slingshot past James. The remaining laps found James glued to the Vipers bumper.

A 2nd place finish was a strong result since James' car had the most rewards weight for that weekend. The Championship is looking better now that the GMG team is leading in the points standings.

   Dino, in the GMG/Stoptech Porsche had another strong car for the race and even ran the fastest race lap, shattering the old track record by several seconds. But early on in the race he was punted by the Viper of Daskalos on lap 1 and was never able to recover due to a bumper that was hanging on the back of the car, relegating Dino to have to pull into the pits to have it removed. He ended up finishing 15, which is not exemplary of his driving performance during the race weekend based on his lap times.

Tomy Drissi, in the GMG/XMEN Porsche had his best result of the season so far, finishing 7th after starting 9th. Tomy had a great start and was as high as 5th, with strong battles from O'Connell, Pilgrim and Roberts but settled into 7th at the end.

Steve Ruiz Jr ran the 4th GMG/Stoptech 997 Cup and finished 12th after starting 18th on the grid. It was his rookie start in the Pirelli World Challenge Series and he held his own against some of the best drivers in the business.

The next race for GMG is at Mosport Park on May 21-21, a double-header weekend for the Pirelli World Challenge. For more information, see gmgracing.com and world-challenge.com.
