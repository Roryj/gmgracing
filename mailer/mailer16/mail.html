Santa Ana, California (August 3rd, 2011) GMG looks to build upon their points lead in the GT category with team owner James Sofronas leading over Patrick Long in the driver standings, as they prepare for rounds 7&8 in the World Challenge Championship being held at Mid-Ohio this coming weekend. James will be helped by teammate and GMG driver Stephen Ruiz and team newcomer Jeff Courtney.

Stephen made his World Challenge debut at Miller Motorsports in May of this year, where he qualified 14th and finished 12th. He will be piloting car # 41, the StopTech/GMG sponsored Porsche 911 GT3 Cup race car this weekend. New to GMG and Porsche Cup Cars, but not to the ultra-competitive series of World Challenge, is Jeff Courtney, hailing from Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
"I'm really excited to be hooked up with GMG and James Sofronas for the season. If anyone knows Porsches, it's GMG," said Courtney, who expects some early challenges but trusts that the GMG squad will make it an easy transition for him. "It is going to be a tough driving technique transition for me, as I have been driving a Viper for the last four years, but I look forward to the challenge" said Courtney.

It will be an adjustment for Courtney, who will be moving from the high-torque, front engine Viper to the more agile, rear engine Porsche.
"GMG is very excited to have Jeff come onboard and race one of our GMG Porsches," said Sofronas. "I have been racing with Jeff for many years and have always considered him a professional on and off the track and look forward to racing up front with him.Â  Jeff has always been competitive in whatever car he has raced but now it's time for him to do it in a Porsche and we are anxious to get him up to speed."

Courtney and the GMG Team expect an enthusiastic fan base at Mid Ohio, including long-time sponsors KENDA Tires and their guests. "It will be terrific having our KENDA supporters there again this year," said Courtney. "They have been a tremendous support for me and I am looking forward to giving them a great race."
William Ziegler, the 2009 World Challenge GT rookie of the year, makes his return to World Challenge racing, in his GMG built; Swisher International sponsored Mitsubishi EVO 10R, after missing events at both Miller and Mosport. The EVO platform is familiar territory for Ziegler, as he finished 2nd at the 2007 T2 runoffs in a Mitsubishi EVO 8.



 

WC Vision has announced that Rounds 7 and 8 of the Pirelli World Challenge Championships will be streamed live online at www.world-challengeTV.com.
Both races as part of the Pirelli World Challenge Mid-Ohio Grand Prix presented by StopTech will be easily accessible to viewers around the world, and will feature audio play-by-play and color commentary from the voice of the series, Greg Creamer. The races will also be available live to Droid phone users. The online feed for Round 7 of the Pirelli World Challenge Sports Car Wars season is scheduled to start at 10:40 a.m. Eastern/ 7:40 a.m. Pacific on Saturday, August 6, while Round 8 will start at 11:25 a.m. Eastern/ 8:25 a.m. Pacific on Sunday, August 7th.
